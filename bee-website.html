Why Bees Matter

Bees are crucial pollinators that help plants reproduce, making them essential for our food supply and ecosystem health. Without bees, many of our favorite fruits and vegetables would cease to exist!

The Busy Life of Bees

A single honey bee can visit up to 5,000 flowers in a single day, playing a crucial role in pollination.

Incredible Communication

Bees communicate through special dances to share information about food sources with their hive mates.

Honey Production

To produce one pound of honey, bees must visit about 2 million flowers and fly over 55,000 miles!

Types of Bees

Honey Bees

Social insects that live in large colonies and are famous for producing honey and beeswax.

Bumble Bees

Large, fuzzy bees that are excellent pollinators and can fly in colder temperatures.

Solitary Bees

Independent bees that make individual nests rather than living in colonies.

How to Help Bees

  • Plant bee-friendly flowers in your garden
  • Avoid using pesticides
  • Provide water sources for bees
  • Support local beekeepers
  • Create bee habitats in your yard
